Rearrange My World
Daniel Caesar
Dear Samu,

I want to start by saying — I did not plan any of this. I did not plan for you to appear, I did not plan for the conversations to feel the way they do, and I certainly did not plan to sit here trying to find words that are worthy of someone like you. But here we are.

Before you, the idea of having a crush on someone felt simple — a passing feeling, something ordinary. Then you came along and quietly rewrote everything I thought I knew about it. You rearranged my world, Samu. Not loudly. Not with grand gestures or dramatic moments. Just... slowly and surely, the way morning light fills a room — until I looked up and everything looked different.

There is something about the way you carry yourself that stays with me long after our conversations end. The gentleness in your voice — soft, unhurried, like it was never in a rush to impress anyone because it never had to be. The way your smile arrives and suddenly there is nowhere else in the world worth looking. The way your eyes hold something — warmth, maybe, or depth — that I find myself thinking about at completely inconvenient times.

You made me realise that the right person does not make your world smaller. They open it up. They add colour to places you did not even notice were grey. That is what talking to you has done for me. I cannot stop smiling — genuinely, uncontrollably, embarrassingly — and I have come to accept that it is entirely your fault.

And today — today — you walk across that stage as someone who spent years studying the architecture of the human mind. You chose one of the most demanding, most beautiful disciplines there is, and you saw it through with grace. A Bachelor of Psychology from Wits is not just a degree. It is proof of who you already were — determined, thoughtful, extraordinary.

So, Samu — congratulations. Not just for graduating. But for being the kind of person who makes the people around her feel more alive just by being present. For the smile that rewrites rooms. For the voice that feels like home. For showing up in my life at a completely unexpected moment and somehow making it feel like exactly the right time.

You rearranged my world. And I would not change a single thing about it.

Yours sincerely,
Steven — The Hopeless Romantic
